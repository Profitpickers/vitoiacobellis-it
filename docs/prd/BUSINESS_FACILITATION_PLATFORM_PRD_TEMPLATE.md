# Business Facilitation Platform PRD Template

# Product Overview

Describe the product or digital platform being created for the represented company, producer, distributor, manufacturer, service provider, or commercial partner.

Include:

- Project name
- Represented organization
- Business sector
- Geographic context
- Commercial role of the platform
- Primary value proposition
- Main contact or facilitator role

The overview should explain what the platform is, who it serves, and why it exists.

# Business Goal

Define the primary business goal of the platform.

Possible goals may include:

- Increase credibility
- Generate qualified commercial leads
- Support B2B introductions
- Facilitate appointment requests
- Present a company or offer to new markets
- Support distributor or buyer acquisition
- Create a professional reference point for commercial conversations

The business goal should be specific enough to guide MVP decisions.

# Target Audience

Describe the commercial audiences the platform is intended to reach.

Include relevant audience segments such as:

- Buyers
- Distributors
- Importers
- Commercial partners
- Investors
- Retail operators
- Ho.Re.Ca. operators
- Strategic introducers
- Service clients

Clarify the audience's likely needs, expectations, and decision-making context.

# User Types

## Producer

Represents a company or organization offering products, services, manufacturing capacity, know-how, or commercial value.

## Buyer

Evaluates whether the represented offer is relevant for purchasing, sourcing, category development, or commercial needs.

## Distributor

Evaluates whether the represented offer fits an existing distribution portfolio, territory, sales network, or customer base.

## Importer

Evaluates whether the represented offer has market fit, compliance potential, commercial appeal, and practical import value.

## Partner

Evaluates possible collaboration, introductions, representation, market development, service delivery, or strategic alignment.

## Investor

Evaluates business potential, credibility, growth opportunity, market positioning, and the possibility of future participation or support.

# User Journeys

Define the main visitor journeys for the platform.

Suggested journey structure:

- Discover the facilitator or represented organization
- Understand the represented company or offer
- Evaluate credibility and relevance
- Watch a video presentation
- Review business resources
- Contact through a direct communication channel
- Request an appointment or conversation
- Decide whether to continue the commercial relationship

Each journey should describe:

- Visitor intent
- Information needed
- Primary action
- Desired outcome

# Core Features

Define the essential MVP features required to support the business goal.

Possible core features:

- Public-facing presentation page
- Digital business card
- Organization or offer overview
- Commercial value proposition
- Trust and credibility section
- Video presentation area
- Resource center
- Appointment request path
- Direct contact call to action
- Mobile responsive layout
- SEO foundation

Only include features that are necessary for the MVP.

# Trust & Credibility Elements

Define the elements needed to make the platform credible for commercial audiences.

Possible elements:

- Clear identity of the facilitator or contact person
- Clear identity of the represented organization
- Professional role description
- Geographic and business context
- Company background
- Product, service, or offer positioning
- Proof points
- Certifications or quality signals
- Commercial references when available
- Responsible and respectful communication style

Trust elements should support human confidence without exaggeration or aggressive sales language.

# Resource Center

Define the business resources that may be useful for qualified visitors.

Possible resources:

- Company profile
- Product catalog
- Service overview
- Commercial presentation
- Price list if appropriate
- Technical sheet
- Certifications
- Media kit
- Case studies
- Market materials

The resource center should help visitors evaluate the opportunity before requesting a conversation.

# Video Presentation

Define the role of video within the platform.

Possible video content:

- Facilitator introduction
- Company presentation
- Product or service overview
- Commercial opportunity explanation
- Market-specific presentation
- Founder or representative message

The video presentation should help build clarity, trust, and human connection.

# Appointment Request

Define how a qualified visitor can request a conversation or appointment.

The MVP appointment request path may be simple and manual.

Describe:

- Primary contact method
- Required visitor information
- Expected response process
- Type of conversation being requested
- Commercial qualification needs

Avoid adding backend, CRM, automation, or scheduling complexity unless explicitly approved for the project.

# Success Criteria

Define how the MVP will be evaluated.

Possible success criteria:

- Clear communication of the represented offer
- Clear contact or appointment request path
- Increased credibility with target audiences
- Qualified conversations generated
- Business resources accessed by relevant contacts
- Video presentation viewed by qualified visitors
- Mobile usability
- SEO readiness
- Maintainability by a No-Code Developer
- Low operational complexity

Success criteria should be tied to practical business outcomes.

# Out Of Scope

List what is explicitly excluded from the MVP.

Possible out-of-scope items:

- CRM
- Database
- Backend implementation
- Dashboard
- Lead scoring
- Relationship scoring
- Trust scoring engine
- Ethical reciprocity scoring
- Automation
- AI assistant
- User accounts
- Login areas
- Payment processing
- E-commerce
- Multi-tenant management
- Advanced analytics
- Complex appointment scheduling

Out-of-scope items should prevent premature technical expansion.
