# Relationship Capital System

## Purpose

The Relationship Capital System is a strategic concept for understanding, organizing, and evaluating the value of commercial relationships over time.

Its purpose is to support better business development decisions by helping identify which relationships, opportunities, introductions, and follow-ups deserve attention, care, and priority.

This document is conceptual only. It does not define an implementation, database, automation, CRM, scoring engine, or operational workflow for the current MVP.

## Entities

The system may eventually consider several types of business entities:

- Producers
- Buyers
- Distributors
- Importers
- Ho.Re.Ca. operators
- Retail chains
- Commercial partners
- Strategic introducers
- Business opportunities
- Commercial matches
- Follow-up activities

Each entity may have different relevance depending on the business context, geography, product category, commercial maturity, and relationship history.

## Status Model

A future status model may help describe the current state of a relationship or opportunity.

Possible relationship statuses:

- New contact
- Qualified contact
- Active conversation
- Introduction made
- Follow-up needed
- Opportunity under evaluation
- Negotiation in progress
- Dormant relationship
- Long-term partner
- Not currently relevant

Possible opportunity statuses:

- Idea
- Open opportunity
- Qualified opportunity
- Match proposed
- Awaiting response
- Meeting requested
- Meeting completed
- In negotiation
- Won
- Lost
- Deferred

These statuses are exploratory and should be refined only if the project reaches a future relationship or opportunity management phase.

## Scoring Ideas

A future system may use lightweight scoring to help prioritize relationships and opportunities.

Possible scoring dimensions:

- Strategic fit
- Commercial potential
- Market relevance
- Product-category alignment
- Geographic relevance
- Urgency
- Relationship strength
- Responsiveness
- Reliability
- Follow-up priority
- Expected opportunity value
- Long-term partnership potential

Scoring should support human judgment, not replace it. Any future score should remain transparent, explainable, and easy for a No-Code Developer or business operator to understand.

## Trust Score

A future trust score may help evaluate the quality and reliability of a business relationship.

Possible trust score signals:

- Clear communication
- Timely responses
- Consistency between words and actions
- Respect for agreements
- Professional behavior
- Transparent expectations
- History of collaboration
- Responsiveness after introductions
- Fair handling of opportunities
- Reputation within the business network

The trust score should not be treated as an absolute judgment of a person or company. It should be used carefully as a business-context indicator, supported by human review and documented reasoning.

## Ethical Reciprocity Index

The Ethical Reciprocity Index is a future concept for evaluating whether a commercial relationship is balanced, respectful, and mutually constructive.

Possible evaluation signals:

- Mutual benefit
- Fair exchange of value
- Respect for introductions
- Recognition of facilitation work
- Transparency in communication
- Responsible use of shared contacts
- Long-term relationship care
- Absence of extractive behavior
- Willingness to create value for both sides

This index should help protect the quality of the commercial network and encourage sustainable relationships. It should never be used mechanically or without context.

## Current Scope Note

The Relationship Capital System is a strategic and exploratory concept.

It is out of scope for the MVP and should not be implemented until the project has completed discovery, product requirements, architecture planning, and explicit approval for a future relationship management phase.
